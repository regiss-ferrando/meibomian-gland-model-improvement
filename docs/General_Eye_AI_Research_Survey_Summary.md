# Eye AI Research Survey Synthesis

**Source workbook:** `Eye_AI_Research_Survey_with_Type.xlsx`

## Key findings

- Total entries: **50**
- Main focus areas:
  - **Diabetic retinopathy** (about 12 papers)
  - **Glaucoma** (about 10 papers)
  - **Cataract** (about 9 papers)
  - **Eye tracking / gaze analysis** (about 7 papers)
- Secondary themes:
  - **Retinal imaging** and disease screening
  - **Myopia prediction**
  - **Segmentation methods**
  - **Virtual reality / head-mounted eye tracking**

## What has already been done

- Strong research exists for AI-based diagnosis of retinal diseases, especially diabetic retinopathy and glaucoma.
- There is also significant work on cataract classification and general eye tracking using deep learning.
- The survey shows a general eye-AI foundation, but not a strong focus on dry eye or tear meniscus analysis.

## Conclusion

The first workbook is a solid general survey of eye AI research after 2012, but it is not yet centered on dry eye detection. For your headset-based dry eye goal, the most relevant next step is to shift focus toward datasets and papers specific to tear meniscus, tear film, meibomian gland, and ocular surface imaging.
