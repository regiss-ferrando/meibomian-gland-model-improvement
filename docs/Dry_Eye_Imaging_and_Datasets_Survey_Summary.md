# Dry Eye Imaging and Datasets Survey Summary

**Source workbook:** `Dry_Eye_Imaging_and_Datasets_Survey_11_entries.xlsx`

## Overview

This focused survey collects the most relevant AI research and dataset resources for dry eye applications centered on:
- tear meniscus segmentation
- tear film analysis
- meibomian gland morphology
- ocular surface imaging

## Contents

- **11 entries total**
- **9 research papers** on dry eye imaging and AI
- **1 dataset entry** for tear meniscus segmentation

## Key topics covered

- Meibomian gland analysis and dropout detection
- Tear meniscus segmentation and measurement
- Tear film breakup time from video
- Ocular surface segmentation using CNNs
- Dry eye severity grading from slit-lamp and ocular images
- Smartphone and eyelid/conjunctival imaging for dry eye screening

## Why this is important

This survey is directly aligned with a headset-based dry eye detection system because it concentrates on imaging targets and datasets that can be captured with near-eye or ocular-surface cameras.

## Recommended focus

1. Prioritize the Figshare tear meniscus segmentation dataset for model training.
2. Combine tear meniscus and tear film imaging with ocular surface segmentation for a more robust dry eye model.
3. Use the meibomian gland and slit-lamp image papers as guides for feature extraction and explainable outputs.

## Notes

This workbook is a narrower, higher-impact follow-up to the broader general eye AI survey, with an explicit shift toward dry eye imaging and actionable dataset resources.
